Overview

Slap Simulator is a VR prototype where you roam a dungeon scene, smack ragdoll NPCs, and rack up points with props you can grab and throw. I built it end-to-end with an OpenXR setup, action-based input, hand animation/collisions, ragdoll tuning, simple scoring UI, and an in-game title splash.

Architecture & implementation

  • Unity (Quest build), OpenXR, XR Interaction Toolkit (action-based input).
  • Custom hand system: animated hand model + capsule colliders per joint + physics-based follow & rotation.
  • Ragdoll characters with joint constraints, friction/bounce materials, and hit reactions.
  • Audio manager for footsteps, impacts, and slap SFX.

Gameplay & systems

  • Hands & collisions. Grip animates a opened and closed fist; colliders on each finger/segment drive physical hits. Follow/rotation use frame-rate-independent motion to reduce jitter.
  • Ragdoll reactions. NPCs idle until hit by a “hand” or tagged prop, then drop into ragdoll.
  • Grab & throw. Heads and props are grabbable; throws register impacts and award points.
  • Scoring. A lightweight AddScore/DisplayScore pair handles points and display.
  • Level & menu. Dungeon environment, splash/title screen, ambient particles and lighting.

Highlights

  • Moved from device-based to action-based XR input for cleaner mappings on Quest.
  • Hand animation + physics colliders (per-joint) for satisfying slap contact.
  • Quaternion rotation follow + deltaTime smoothing to reduce jitter and startup drift.
